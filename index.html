Artist Statement

Through wheel-thrown forms, I explore the intersection of simplicity, utility, and presence. My practice is grounded in repetition and refinement—an ongoing conversation between hand, material, and function. I work primarily with stoneware, often drawn to subdued, natural glazes that highlight the form rather than distract from it.

My pieces are informed by an interest in slowness and sensory experience. I approach pottery as a quiet counterpoint to overstimulation—a way to create space for pause, care, and connection. The work invites use but does not demand attention. It is meant to live alongside the rituals of daily life, supporting them without interrupting them.
