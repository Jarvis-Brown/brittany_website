About Me

I first touched clay during a beginner’s wheel class on a date. I didn’t expect it to stick, but that two-hour session turned into a quiet obsession. Since then, I’ve kept returning to the wheel. Today, I split my time between a small home studio and a local community space, where I continue to learn and shape each piece by hand.

I grew up in Brooklyn, where stillness had to be created, not found. I’ve always found calm in caring for houseplants and surrounding myself with natural textures. Working with clay gives me that same sense of quiet focus. It invites patience, presence, and care. These values show up in my work, which leans toward simplicity and function. I’m drawn to soft forms, muted glazes, and earthy palettes that reflect the quiet beauty of everyday life.

My pieces are shaped by intention and made with the hope that they bring a sense of ease into the moments that matter most. Whether it's a quiet morning or a shared meal, I'm grateful when something I've made becomes part of someone else's rhythm.
